All about Dandelions

"Though dandelion today is considered by most people to be a useless weed, in truth it is one of the most beneficial and healthful of herbs. Every part of the dandelion has a use, ranging from food, to medicine, to dye. These beneficial properties did not always go unnoticed in North America: Up until the 1800s, people would actually pull grass out of their yards to make room for dandelions and other useful "weeds" such as chickweed, malva, and chamomile."(1)

(1) from the book ("Dandelion Medicine: Remedies and Recipes to Detoxify, Nourish, Stimulate" - Brigitte Mars)
Misc. Information on Dandelions
"Islamic physcian Ibn sina prescribed dandelion root to stimulate bile production for this with liver problems."(1)
"Dandelion grows where the soil is healthy - it is considered an indicator of the presence of potassium, magnesium, calcium, and sodium"(1)
"Medicinally, dandelions can be used fresh or prepared as a tea, tincture, encapsulated powder, juice, or homeopathic formula"(1)
"Dandelions provide a multitude of culinary possibilities... young leaves are most commonly used and should be gathered before the flower stalk acheives full height"(1)
"One popular legend ascribes dandelion's birth to the work of fairies. Many thousands of years ago, when the world was populated with fairies and elves, the first human arrived. They soon caused these tiny creatures many problems, as the humans were usually unable to see the wee folk and would step on them. So the fairies took to dressing in bright yellow garments and eventually were changed into dandelions, which have the ability to spring back up if trodden upon. Thus, it is believed that dandelions recover so quickly from being stepped on because each contains the spirit of a fairy"(1)
